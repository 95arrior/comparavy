# AteFlo Korean Product UI

## 1. Visual identity

AteFlo should feel:

- clean
- Korean product-service friendly
- Toss-like in flow simplicity
- Google-like in whitespace and calmness
- white/light background
- clear hierarchy
- low text density
- one action at a time

AteFlo should not feel:

- blue-heavy AI template
- dark neon SaaS
- shortcut directory
- blog/listing site
- generic card grid
- overdesigned dashboard
- prompt marketplace

## 2. Layout principles

- Do not reuse the old stacked marketing homepage as the visible product foundation.
- Do not lead with giant product explanation blocks.
- Do not use a card grid first unless the cards are the primary guided action.
- Do not repeat white cards on beige background as the main rhythm.
- Do not use a shortcut search hero.
- Do not show Featured Shortcuts on the product homepage.
- The first screen should be calm and direct.
- Use one question, five choices, and a clear next action as the homepage rhythm.
- The assembly page should feel like chat guidance, not a form.
- The dashboard preview should feel like locked execution buttons, not an article list.

## 3. Copy principles

- Korean-first.
- Short sentences.
- No jargon.
- Use words like `실행 패키지`, `세팅`, `진단`, and `전체 패키지`.
- Do not overuse `키트` without explaining what the user can do.
- Do not claim guaranteed sales, rankings, leads, customers, bookings, income, or growth.
