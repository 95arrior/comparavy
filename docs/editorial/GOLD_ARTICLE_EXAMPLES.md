# Gold Article Examples

These are outlines, not full articles. They show the difference between solving a workflow and ranking tools.

## Example 1: How-To

Title: How to Turn Meeting Notes Into a Client Recap with AI

### Strong First 100 Words

To turn meeting notes into a client recap with AI, start by cleaning the notes, then ask AI to extract decisions, action items, owners, dates, open questions, and risks. Do not ask for a polished email first. First get a structured recap you can verify against the notes. After the facts are checked, ask for a client-ready version with a short summary, bullets for commitments, and a clear next step. This workflow works best when AI drafts the structure and you approve every claim before sending.

### Real-World Scenario

A freelancer finishes a 45-minute client call with rough notes, three decisions, two open questions, and several follow-up tasks. They need a concise recap email before the client forgets what was agreed.

### What You Need

- Meeting notes or transcript.
- Client name and project context.
- Known decisions.
- Action items and owners.
- Dates or deadlines.
- Tone preference for the client.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

1. Clean the notes.
   Remove side chatter, mark unclear names, and keep the original transcript nearby.

2. Extract the source facts.
   Ask AI for decisions, action items, owners, dates, blockers, and open questions. Output should be a structured checklist, not an email yet.

3. Verify the checklist.
   Compare each item against the notes. Fix owners, dates, and commitments before rewriting.

4. Draft the client recap.
   Ask for a short email with a summary, agreed decisions, action items, open questions, and next meeting step.

5. Tighten and send.
   Remove internal notes, soften uncertain language, and make sure every ask is clear.

### Mobile Workflow

Use mobile when the notes are already clean and you only need a quick recap draft or final approval. Keep the recap short and avoid heavy source checking on a phone.

### Desktop Workflow

Use desktop when you need the transcript, AI draft, and final email side by side. Desktop is better for checking owners, dates, and missing context.

### Tools You Can Use

- Otter.ai: useful when the meeting transcript is the source.
- Fireflies: useful when action items and searchable meeting records matter.
- ChatGPT or Claude: useful for turning a verified checklist into a polished client email.

### Example Result

Subject: Recap and next steps from today's kickoff

Hi Taylor, here is the short recap from today's call. We agreed to focus the first draft on the landing page offer, keep the timeline to two review rounds, and send source assets by Friday. I own the first copy draft by Tuesday. You own brand examples and product screenshots by Friday. Open question: should the pricing section mention the annual plan?

### Common Mistakes

- Asking for a polished email before extracting decisions.
- Sending AI-invented owners or dates.
- Removing open questions because they feel messy.
- Forgetting to check the recap against the original notes.

### FAQ Examples

Q: What should I ask AI to pull from meeting notes?

A: Ask for decisions, action items, owners, due dates, open questions, risks, and anything the client explicitly approved.

Q: Can I do this from my phone?

A: Yes, if the notes are already clean. Use desktop when the transcript is long or the recap affects deadlines, budget, or client commitments.

Q: Should I use a meeting transcription tool or a chatbot?

A: Use the transcription tool to capture and search the meeting. Use a chatbot after the notes are clean and you need a client-ready recap.

### Final Next Step

Run the workflow on one real meeting. If you are unsure which tool fits your meeting style, use /finder to compare options by transcript source, budget, and review depth.

## Example 2: Tool-Decision

Title: Best AI Tools for Turning Meeting Notes Into Client Recaps

### Quick Verdict

Start with Otter.ai if your main problem is turning a live meeting or transcript into a structured recap. Choose Fireflies if your team needs searchable meeting records and action-item tracking. Use ChatGPT or Claude after the source facts are checked, because they are better for polishing the recap email than for proving what happened in the meeting.

### Which One Should You Choose?

- If you need transcript-first recap help, choose Otter.ai.
- If you need team meeting records and follow-up tracking, choose Fireflies.
- If you already have clean notes and need a polished email, choose ChatGPT or Claude.
- If the recap includes commitments, deadlines, or money, verify the source before sending.

### Comparison Criteria

- Source capture quality.
- Action item extraction.
- Owner and date clarity.
- Email polish.
- Search and team handoff.
- Mobile review.
- Desktop review depth.
- Cost fit for recurring meetings.

### Best Picks by Situation

- Best for solo consultants: Otter.ai, because the transcript and recap start in one place.
- Best for teams: Fireflies, because searchable records and follow-up tracking matter more.
- Best for polished client wording: ChatGPT or Claude, after the facts are verified.

### Tool-Card Examples

Otter.ai

- Best for: solo calls where the transcript is the source and the recap needs decisions, owners, and next steps.
- Avoid if: you already have clean notes and only need final email wording.
- Watch for: transcript errors in names, dates, and commitments.

Fireflies

- Best for: recurring client or team meetings where search, records, and action-item tracking matter.
- Avoid if: you only need a one-off recap and do not want another meeting system.
- Watch for: action items that sound right but do not match the client's exact wording.

ChatGPT or Claude

- Best for: turning verified notes into a clean client email.
- Avoid if: you expect it to know what happened without source notes.
- Watch for: invented certainty, missing open questions, or softened deadlines.

### High-Intent FAQ

Q: Which tool should I use if I only have rough notes?

A: Use ChatGPT or Claude only after you label decisions, owners, dates, and open questions. If you need transcript capture, use Otter.ai or Fireflies first.

Q: Which tool is safest for client commitments?

A: The safest workflow is source-first. Use a transcription or notes tool for the record, then use a chatbot to polish only after you verify the commitments.

Q: Which option is better on mobile?

A: Mobile is fine for reviewing a short recap or approving a draft. Use desktop for transcript checking and side-by-side comparison.

### Final Verdict

For most solo client calls, start with Otter.ai. For teams that need searchable meeting history, start with Fireflies. For final email polish, use ChatGPT or Claude after the facts are checked. If your workflow depends on meeting source, budget, or device, use /finder before adding another subscription.

## Workflow vs Ranking

The how-to article teaches the reader how to produce a client recap. The tool-decision article helps the reader choose which tool belongs in that recap workflow. If a how-to guide spends most of its space ranking tools, it fails. If a tool-decision guide does not give a clear choice, it fails.
